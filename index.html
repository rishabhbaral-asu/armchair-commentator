NCAA Men's Basketball

Devils Dominate in Desert Duel

By Jordan Cross | Sports Desk

TEMPE — The Sun Devils orchestrated a masterclass in defensive pressure Saturday night, routing the visiting Colorado Buffaloes 78–61 at Desert Financial Arena. What began as a tightly contested tactical battle in the first half exploded into a runaway victory as ASU’s backcourt found their rhythm.

Leading by only four at the break, Arizona State opened the second half with a blistering 14–2 run. The Buffaloes, plagued by 18 turnovers, simply could not handle the Devils' transition offense. The student section erupted in the final minutes as the walk-ons cleared the bench, capping a night that solidified the Devils' standing in the conference race.

However, Indian captain Suryakumar Yadav stabilized the ship with a masterful unbeaten 84, dragging India to a defensible 161.

In reply, the USA stayed in the hunt with a 50-run partnership between Milind Kumar and Sanjay Krishnamurthi, but the spin choke of Axar Patel and the death bowling of Mohammed Siraj eventually stifled the chase, handing India a 29-run victory.
