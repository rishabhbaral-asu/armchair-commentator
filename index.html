ALTITUDE SICKNESS

Devils fade late in Boulder; Colorado closes on 12-4 run to seal 78-70 victory. By THE SPORTS DESK
Boulder remains a house of horrors for the Sun Devils. In a game defined by thin air and thinner margins, Arizona State (12-12, 3-8 Big 12) withered in the final four minutes at the CU Events Center.

Despite a heroic 23-point effort from point guard Moe Odum, who fearlessly attacked the paint against Colorado's towering frontcourt, the Devils simply ran out of gas. With the game tied 66-66 at the 4:10 mark, Colorado's Barrington Hargress (23 pts) took over, hitting a contested mid-range jumper that sparked the decisive run. The Buffaloes converted six consecutive free throws in the final minute to ice the game.

"We expended so much energy digging out of that early hole," Head Coach Bobby Hurley said, his tie loosened and voice raspy. "When you're playing at 5,000 feet against a team that rebounds like that, you can't afford empty possessions in crunch time."

VEGAS INSIDER

Where the Sharp Money is Going

The line opened at Seattle -3.5 and was quickly bet up to -4.5. The public loves the Patriots underdog story, but the sharps are looking at Seattle's defensive DVOA rankings. The Seahawks rank #1 against the pass, a nightmare scenario for a young QB like Maye in his first Super Bowl.

Prediction: Defense travels. The Patriots have had a magical run, beating Denver in the snow to get here, but the Seahawks are playing efficient, mistake-free football. Darnold manages the game, Kenneth Walker III controls the clock, and Seattle hoists the Lombardi.

Hoops Analysis: Paint Problems

The Breakdown: Colorado out-rebounded ASU 42-28. This singular stat decided the game. While ASU forced 14 turnovers and shot a respectable 45% from the field, they gave up 16 second-chance points. The Buffaloes' 6'11" sophomore Sebastian Rancik (17 pts, 11 reb) simply camped in the lane, forcing ASU's guards to collapse and leaving shooters open on the perimeter.

THE TURNING POINT

3:30 Remaining • Tied 68-68

ASU committed back-to-back offensive fouls—one a moving screen by Diop, the other a charge by Johnson. Colorado capitalized immediately with a corner three from Bangot Dak and a transition dunk. That 5-point swing in 40 seconds broke ASU's rhythm permanently.

Coach's Quote: "We liked the pace. We liked the shots we got. But you can't beat a team like Colorado at altitude if you don't box out on free throws. We gave them three extra possessions in the last two minutes. That's the ballgame." — Bobby Hurley

Ice Report: The Wall of St. Cloud

The Breakdown: For 40 minutes, St. Cloud State put on a defensive clinic, trapping ASU in the neutral zone (1-3-1 forecheck) and forcing turnovers at the blue line. But the third period saw a different Sun Devil team. Coach Powers shifted lines, loading up the top unit, which resulted in two quick goals to cut the deficit to 4-3.

The final minute was pure chaos at Mullett Arena. With the goalie pulled, ASU fired four shots in the last 15 seconds. SCSU goalie Patriks Berzins made a sprawling pad save on Bennett Schimek right as the buzzer sounded, preserving the win and silencing the student section.

TACTICAL NOTE

The Royal Road

ASU struggled all night to move the puck laterally across the center of the offensive zone (the "Royal Road"). Berzins is a positional goalie; if you don't make him move East-West, he stops everything. ASU's third-period goals both came from cross-ice passes. Lesson learned too late.

Diamond Report: Total Domination

The Breakdown: This was a statement. Northwestern, usually a Big Ten powerhouse, looked lost against the rise-ball of ASU ace Kenzie Brown. She struck out 8 of the first 9 batters she faced. Offensively, the Sun Devils were aggressive early in the count, hunting fastballs.

KEY SEQUENCE

Bottom 3rd Inning • 0 Outs

Leading just 3-0, ASU loaded the bases on two walks and a bunt single. Senior Captain Yannira Acuña then ripped a bases-clearing double into the left-center gap. The exit velocity was 104 mph. That swing broke the game open and demoralized the Wildcats.

The Mercy Rule: Enacted when a team leads by 8 or more runs after 5 innings. By ending the game early, ASU saved their bullpen arms for tomorrow's double-header against Oklahoma State. "Efficiency is the name of the game in February," Coach Ford said.

World Cup: SKY's Rescue Act

The Breakdown: The USA had India on the ropes at 77/6. The Wankhede Stadium was silent. But Suryakumar Yadav (84* off 48) played an innings of pure genius, dragging India to a competitive 161. His 360-degree play manipulated the field settings, frustrating the American bowlers who had started so well with the new ball.

In response, the USA chase never got out of second gear. Axar Patel (2/16) and Ravi Bishnoi (1/19) applied the "spin choke" in the middle overs, raising the required rate from 8.0 to 14.0 by the 15th over.

MATCH TURNING POINT

The 18th Over

Ali Khan attempted to bowl yorkers to SKY but missed his length twice. Yadav ramped the first over fine leg for six and carved the second over point for another maximum. 19 runs came off the over, shifting the momentum entirely and pushing the total from "defendable" to "winning."
