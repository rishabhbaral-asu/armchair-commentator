SCSU Silences Mullett Arena; Devils Eyes Rematch

Tyson Gross nets two as Sun Devils fail to capitalize on 34-shot barrage in NCHC opener.

The atmosphere at Mullett Arena was electric, but the results were ice-cold for the Arizona State Sun Devils. Despite a relentless offensive effort that tested sophomore goaltender Patriks Berzins 34 times, ASU fell 4-1 to St. Cloud State in a physical Friday night showdown. The Huskies utilized a disciplined structural trap that effectively neutralized the Devils' transition game. Tyson Gross proved to be the primary antagonist, netting a shorthanded goal in the second period before adding a back-breaker in the third. Tonight, Coach Greg Powers’ squad faces a pivotal "split or quit" moment as they attempt to solve Berzins and find the back of the net...

The International Desk

Bumrah Fever Sidelined; Siraj Returns for Wankhede Clash

India vs USA: World Cup Title Defense Begins with Toss Loss.

In a shock to the Indian camp, Jasprit Bumrah has been ruled out of today's opener against the USA due to a sudden fever. Mohammed Siraj, returning to the T20I fold after 557 days, steps into the primary pace role. USA captain Monank Patel has won the toss and opted to bowl, looking to exploit early Mumbai sea breezes.

The Anatomy of a Breakdown

Last night's 4-1 loss was a tale of two efficiencies. While ASU controlled the puck for significant stretches, the Huskies were surgical. Tyson Gross’s first goal came during an ASU power play—a deflection off a skater that allowed Finn Loftus to feed Gross for a tap-in past Samuel Urban. It was a momentum shift from which the Devils never fully recovered. Noah Powell managed to level the score at 1-1 with a shorthanded goal of his own, assisted by Logan Morrell and Samuel Alfano, but the offense stalled thereafter.

Defensively, the Sun Devils struggled with SCSU’s net-front presence. Austin Burnevik and Verner Miettinen added empty-net goals to "ice" the game, but it was the 33-save performance by Berzins that stood as the wall ASU couldn't climb. For tonight’s rematch, the Devils must improve their 0-for-2 power play conversion and find a way to penetrate the slot rather than settling for perimeter shots.

Mumbai Homecoming: Netravalkar Returns to the Wankhede

For USA’s Saurabh Netravalkar, today’s match is more than a World Cup fixture—it is a return to his roots. Born and raised in Mumbai, Netravalkar represented India at the 2010 U19 World Cup alongside stars like KL Rahul. Now, the Oracle software engineer leads the American attack against his native land on the very ground where he once sought to make his name.

The tactical battle shifted at the toss. Monank Patel’s decision to bowl first puts the pressure on India’s new-look opening pair of Abhishek Sharma and Ishan Kishan. With Suryakumar Yadav leading at his home ground, India will look to set a target beyond the reach of Andries Gous and the American middle order, who were frustrated by the "two-paced" nature of the pitch in early sessions.
