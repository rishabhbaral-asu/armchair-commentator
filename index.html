NCHC
Showdown.

ASU Hockey returns to Mullett Arena following a split at Colorado College, facing a St. Cloud State team making its first-ever trip to Tempe.

The narrative centers on Bennett Schimek. Currently leading the NCHC in assists (27), Schimek is within striking distance of the program's single-season points record. His synergy with Cruz Lucius has made the Sun Devils the only team in the country with two players over the 110-career point mark. Defensively, ASU leans on Samuel Urban, whose Mike Richter Award watchlist inclusion is well-earned after eight games of 30+ saves this season.

St. Cloud State arrives desperate for a bounce-back. After being swept by Miami (OH), the Huskies are leaning on sophomore Austin Burnevik (15 goals) and faceoff specialist Tyson Gross, who leads the NCAA in draws won. The Huskies' discipline in the faceoff circle will be the primary obstacle to ASU's puck possession game.

ASU 4 - 2 SCSU

Wankhede
Return.

India begins its T20 title defense against a USA side that functionally feels like a "Mumbai B" team due to its local connections.

The late injury to Harshit Rana has forced a tactical shift, with Mohammed Siraj joining the squad to exploit the Wankhede’s evening swing. India’s transition away from the Rohit-Virat era is complete; the focus is now on Abhishek Sharma's explosive powerplay intent and Suryakumar Yadav's 360-degree dominance on his home ground.

USA’s Saurabh Netravalkar returns to the ground where he spent his formative years. Alongside Shubham Ranjane, he represents a USA unit that understands Mumbai's "red soil" bounce better than most associate nations. Their primary challenge will be surviving Jasprit Bumrah’s opening spell to set a platform for Andries Gous and Monank Patel.
