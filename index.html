Solving the Berzins Puzzle

Tonight's Preview: Scouting the Huskies and the ASU Power Play.

Tonight’s rematch at the Mullett isn't just about effort—it’s about geometry. On Friday, St. Cloud State effectively pushed ASU’s shooters to the perimeter, resulting in high shot counts but low-quality scoring chances. To salvage a series split, the Sun Devils must penetrate the "Royal Road"—the imaginary line bisecting the slot—to force Patriks Berzins to move laterally.

The Sun Devils' power play, which went 0-for-2 last night, will be the X-factor. With Bennett Schimek and Cruz Lucius operating the half-wall, ASU needs more than just puck movement; they need traffic in front of the crease to disrupt Berzins' sightlines. Defensively, Samuel Urban will look to bounce back. While he wasn't at fault for the odd-man rushes on Friday, his ability to steal a goal in the first period could be the psychological edge the Sun Devils need to break the Husky momentum.

2026 World Cup: The Mumbai Stand-Off

The 2026 T20 World Cup begins at the Wankhede Stadium with a script that feels more like a homecoming than an international clash. For USA’s Saurabh Netravalkar, bowling at his former home ground provides a unique advantage; he understands the Mumbai "red soil" bounce better than any other associate player.

India’s strategy, led by Suryakumar Yadav at his domestic base, revolves around sheer power. Despite the absence of Bumrah, the Indian attack led by Arshdeep Singh and a returning Mohammed Siraj remains formidable. The USA will rely on the middle-order stability of Milind Kumar and Harmeet Singh to weather the early storm if India sets a target north of 180.
