Anatomy of the Rematch

Friday night's 4-1 scoreline was deceptive. ASU outshot St. Cloud 34-18, yet the Huskies' defensive structure remained unbroken. The key for tonight lies in the Sun Devils' power play (0-for-2 on Friday). With Bennett Schimek and Cruz Lucius operating the half-wall, the objective is to pull the SCSU defenders out of the low slot.

Defensively, ASU must account for Tyson Gross. His ability to leak behind the defense for odd-man rushes was the difference maker in the opener. Expect Samuel Urban to start in net again; despite the loss, his tracking of the puck remained sharp through two periods.

The Wankhede Homecoming

For Saurabh Netravalkar, this match is a full-circle moment. Playing on the Mumbai soil where he was raised, the USA's leading seamer faces an Indian lineup that has been forced to adapt. The loss of Bumrah is massive for India, but it provides a platform for Mohammed Siraj to prove he is still the king of the white-ball opener.

The USA middle order, featuring Harmeet Singh and Milind Kumar, will be tested by the spin-twins Ravi Bishnoi and Varun Chakravarthy on a pitch that is expected to grip as the evening progresses.
