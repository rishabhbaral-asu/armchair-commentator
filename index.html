Men's Basketball

ALTITUDE SICKNESS IN BOULDER

Devils fade late at CU Events Center, falling 78-70 despite offensive spark.
Boulder remains a house of horrors for the Sun Devils. In a game defined by runs, Arizona State (12-12, 3-8 Big 12) couldn't withstand the final surge from the Colorado Buffaloes, dropping a hard-fought 78-70 contest on the road. The Devils held a slim lead with eight minutes remaining, thanks to aggressive penetration from the guards, but Colorado's size advantage in the paint ultimately wore down the smaller ASU lineup.

"We expended so much energy digging out of that early hole," Head Coach Bobby Hurley said post-game. "When you're playing at 5,000 feet, you can't afford empty possessions in crunch time." Colorado closed the game on a 12-4 run, converting six consecutive free throws to ice the game. The loss puts ASU's tournament hopes on life support, making next week's homestand a mandatory sweep.
NCHC Hockey

Sweep at the Mullett

FINAL: St. Cloud State 4, ASU 3

A frantic third-period rally wasn't enough to save the weekend. Down 4-1, goals from Schimek and Lucius brought the 5,000 fans to their feet, but SCSU goaltender Patriks Berzins made a sprawling save at the buzzer to deny overtime. The Sun Devils have now lost four straight conference games.
NCAA Softball

Mercy Rule Mastery

FINAL: ASU 8, Northwestern 0 (5 Innings)

Kenzie Brown was nearly untouchable, and the Sun Devil bats woke up in a big way at Farrington Stadium. A five-run 3rd inning broke the game open, culminating in a walk-off double that invoked the mercy rule. It was a complete performance against a ranked opponent.
International Cricket

MUMBAI: INDIA SURVIVES USA SCARE

Suryakumar Yadav rescues hosts after top-order collapse; USA falls short by 29 runs.
In a match that was closer than the scorecard suggests, India defeated the USA by 29 runs to open their 2026 World Cup campaign. Suryakumar Yadav was the savior for the hosts, smashing a captain's knock of 84* after the American pacers reduced India to 77/6. The USA chase started brightly with Andries Gous, but crumbled against the spin of Axar Patel (2/16) in the middle overs. "We showed we belong on this stage," USA captain Monank Patel said, "but SKY was the difference today."

Hoops Analysis: Paint Problems

The Breakdown: Colorado out-rebounded ASU 42-28, a stat that tells the entire story. While ASU forced 14 turnovers, they gave up 16 second-chance points. The Buffaloes' big men simply camped in the lane, forcing ASU's guards to collapse and leaving shooters open on the perimeter.

Turning Point: With 3:30 left and the game tied 68-68, ASU committed back-to-back offensive fouls. Colorado capitalized with a corner three and a dunk, swinging momentum permanently. "Discipline in the final four minutes is non-negotiable," Hurley noted.

Coach's Corner

"We liked the pace. We liked the shots we got. We just didn't like the result on the glass. You can't beat a team like Colorado if you don't box out on free throws." — Bobby Hurley

Ice Recap: The Late Surge

The Breakdown: For 40 minutes, St. Cloud State put on a defensive clinic, trapping ASU in the neutral zone and forcing turnovers. But the third period saw a different Sun Devil team. Coach Powers shifted lines, loading up the top unit, which resulted in two quick goals. The Mullett was rocking, but the clock was the ultimate enemy.

Key Stat: 1-for-5 on the Power Play. In a one-goal game, the failure to convert on a 5-minute major in the second period haunts the Devils.

Tactical Note

ASU finally started moving the puck laterally across the "Royal Road" in the third period. This forced Berzins to move East-West, opening up the five-hole. Too little, too late, but a blueprint for next week.

World Cup: SKY's Rescue Act

The Breakdown: The USA had India on the ropes at 77/6. The Wankhede was silent. But Suryakumar Yadav (84 off 48) played an innings of pure class, dragging India to 161. His 360-degree play manipulated the field settings, frustrating the American bowlers who had started so well. USA's chase never got out of second gear against the spin choke.

Key Moment: The 18th over. SKY hit three sixes off Ali Khan, shifting the momentum entirely and pushing the total from "defendable" to "winning."

Pitch Report

The dew factor didn't play as big a role as expected. The pitch slowed down in the second innings, making Axar Patel's arm-balls lethal.
