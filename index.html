The Quarterback Clash

Santa Clara, CA — It is a battle of narratives as much as football. On one sideline stands Sam Darnold, the once-discarded prospect who found salvation in Seattle. Under Mike Macdonald's system, Darnold threw for 4,200 yards and 32 touchdowns this season, unlocking the terrifying potential of DK Metcalf and Jaxon Smith-Njigba.

Opposite him is the future: Drake Maye. The Patriots' sophomore sensation has evoked memories of Brady, leading New England back to the promised land in just his second year. Maye's connection with rookie wideout Rome Odunze has been lethal in the playoffs.

"We know who they are," Patriots coach Jerod Mayo said yesterday. "They want to stretch the field. Our job is to make Sam play small ball."

Key Matchup: Patriots CB Christian Gonzalez vs. Seahawks WR DK Metcalf. If Gonzalez can leave Metcalf on an island, New England can stack the box against Kenneth Walker III.

The Chance Stephens Game

I

t's not just another Big Ten matchup at Williams Arena. Today, Minnesota (14-7) hosts Maryland (14-8) in a game circled on the calendar of one man: Chance Stephens. The sharpshooting guard, who transferred from Maryland to Minnesota for the 2025-26 season, faces his former teammates for the first time.

Stephens has been a revelation for the Gophers, averaging 12.4 points and shooting 41% from deep. But he faces a Maryland defense anchored by sophomore sensation Derik Queen. Queen has been a force in the paint, averaging a double-double (16.5 ppg, 10.2 rpg).

"It's all love," Stephens said at shootaround. "But I want this one. Badly."

Key to the Game: Can Minnesota senior Cade Tyson stretch the floor enough to pull Queen out of the paint? If Maryland controls the rebounding battle, they win.

Silva Takes the Circle

The Sun Devils (4-0) look to close out the Kajikawa Classic with a perfect record as they host the Memphis Tigers this afternoon.

All eyes are on ASU's senior ace Aissa Silva. The Tucson native has been unhittable early in the 2026 campaign, boasting a 0.89 ERA. She matches up against a Memphis lineup struggling for consistency. Memphis senior pitcher Rylee Dugar will need the game of her life to stifle an ASU offense led by Tanya Windle (batting .450).

"We want to finish the weekend strong," Silva said. "Defense has been amazing behind me."

Buffs Stampede Late

Despite 23 points from Moe Odum, the Sun Devils fell 78-70 at the CU Events Center. The turning point came with 3:12 remaining. Tied at 66, Colorado went on a 12-4 run fueled by offensive rebounds. Colorado's big men simply wore down ASU's freshman center Massamba Diop.

Junior guard Bryce Ford added 12 points for ASU but struggled from deep (2-for-8). "We have to learn how to close on the road," Ford said postgame.

India Avoids Disaster

MUMBAI — The 2026 T20 World Cup nearly saw its biggest upset on day one. The USA bowling attack, led by Saurabh Netravalkar, reduced India to a stunning 46/4 inside the powerplay at Wankhede Stadium. A silenced crowd watched in horror.

Enter Captain Suryakumar Yadav. The "SKY" exploded for an unbeaten 84 off 48 balls, using his trademark scoop shots to manipulate the field. He partnered with Hardik Pandya (32) to drag India to 161/9.

In response, USA fought bravely, reaching 132/8, but the spin duo of Kuldeep Yadav and Axar Patel proved too much on the turning track. India wins by 29 runs.

Berzins Robs Devils

St. Cloud State goalie Patriks Berzins was the difference maker Saturday night at Mullett Arena. The Latvian netminder turned away 38 shots, including a miraculous glove save in the dying seconds to preserve a 4-3 victory for the Huskies.

ASU trailed 4-1 entering the third but mounted a furious comeback. Goals by Bennett Schimek and a power-play blast cut the lead to one, but they could not find the equalizer. St. Cloud's Veeti Miettinen scored twice for the visitors.

Mercy Rule in Tempe

Arizona State needed only five innings to dispatch Northwestern 8-0. Pitcher Kenzie Brown was efficient, throwing a complete game two-hitter. The offense was sparked by senior Tanya Windle, who cleared the bases with a double in the 3rd inning to break the game open.

Freshman JazMarie Roberts also impressed, going 2-for-3 with an RBI triple. The Sun Devils are now 4-0.
