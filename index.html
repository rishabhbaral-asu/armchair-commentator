NCHC
Showdown.

ASU Hockey returns to Mullett Arena following a split at Colorado College, facing a St. Cloud State team making its first-ever trip to Tempe.

The narrative centers on Bennett Schimek. Currently leading the NCHC in assists, Schimek is within striking distance of the program's single-season points record. His synergy with Cruz Lucius has made the Sun Devils a dual-threat nightmare on the power play. Defensively, ASU leans on Samuel Urban, whose recent form has anchored a team that often sacrifices defensive structure for offensive transition speed.

St. Cloud State arrives looking to stabilize after a difficult run. The Huskies are leaning on Austin Burnevik to find the back of the net, while Tyson Gross will be critical in the faceoff circle to deny ASU the puck possession they crave. If SCSU can withstand the initial "Mullett Magic" surge, their veteran blue line may be able to grind out a low-scoring result.

Score Prediction

ASU 4 - 2 SCSU

Expect an empty-netter from Schimek to seal a historic night in Tempe.

Wankhede
Return.

India begins its T20 title defense at the Wankhede, a venue where the toss dictates the tactical blueprint.

With the 7:30 PM IST (7:00 AM AZT) start, the "Dew Factor" will be the primary protagonist. India’s captain, Suryakumar Yadav, is expected to exploit this by bowling first, forcing the USA to set a target with a dry ball before the Mumbai humidity makes the second-innings chase much easier for Indian batsmen. The inclusion of Mohammed Siraj provides the necessary early-innings swing to rattle the USA top order before the moisture sets in.

For the USA, Saurabh Netravalkar will need to bowl the spell of his life during the Powerplay. If the USA can post a 160+ score, they might hope for Harmeet Singh and Nosthush Kenjige to extract some early-innings purchase from the pitch before the ball starts sliding through in the chase.

Toss Prediction

India wins the toss and chooses to FIELD first.
