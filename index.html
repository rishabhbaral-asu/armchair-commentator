NCHC
Showdown.

The battle for the desert begins as Arizona State hosts St. Cloud State in a critical conference clash.

Arizona State enters the weekend looking to leverage home-ice advantage at Mullett Arena. The Sun Devils have shown a penchant for explosive offensive bursts, led by senior **Bennett Schimek**. Schimek's ability to navigate tight defensive zones has him on track for a record-breaking season, but he faces a disciplined St. Cloud State unit that prioritizes high-percentage shot blocking. The Huskies' veteran presence, particularly **Josh Zinger** on the blue line, will be tasked with neutralizing ASU's speed on the wings.

Goaltending remains the x-factor. **Samuel Urban** has been a pillar for the Sun Devils, often bailing out an aggressive defensive corps that likes to join the rush. St. Cloud State, meanwhile, relies on a balanced four-line attack to wear down opponents. If ASU can capitalize on early power-play opportunities, they may force SCSU out of their defensive shell.

Wankhede
Return.

A high-stakes Group A opener sees the defending champions meet the rising American force.

India's strategy at the Wankhede is clear: use raw pace to disrupt the USA's top order. With **Mohammed Siraj** replacing **Harshit Rana**, India gains an experienced operator who understands Mumbai's swing conditions. **Suryakumar Yadav** leads a side that is transitioning away from the Rohit-Virat era, placing immense pressure on **Abhishek Sharma** to provide the fireworks.

The USA isn't just an underdog; they are a squad full of local knowledge. **Saurabh Netravalkar**, formerly of Mumbai, knows every crack in this pitch. The inclusion of **Shubham Ranjane**, another Mumbai domestic veteran, gives the USA a "home-away-from-home" advantage that could bridge the talent gap.
