Knights' Cochran Stifles Terps' Bats

FINAL: UCF 5, Maryland 2

ORLANDO, FL — The UCF Knights (4-1) continued their dominance in the Black & Gold Classic, relying on the arm of pitcher Katelyn Cochran to secure a victory over Maryland.

Maryland's sophomore catcher Zayda Rocke tried to rally the troops, delivering a vocal performance behind the plate and an RBI double in the 6th inning. However, UCF infielder Aubrey Evans proved too much to handle, going 3-for-4 with two runs scored.

Maryland two-way player Elisea Wiegand took the loss in the circle but showed promise at first base, turning an unassisted double play in the 4th. The Terps (2-3) will look to bounce back next weekend.

Silva Spins Gem for Sun Devils

FINAL: Arizona State 4, Memphis 1

TEMPE, Ariz. — Senior ace Aissa Silva was the story of the day at Farrington Stadium. The Arizona transfer struck out 10 Memphis batters, cementing her role as the Sun Devils' true No. 1 option for the 2026 campaign.

Memphis senior pitcher Rylee Dugar kept the Tigers in the game early, changing speeds effectively. But in the 5th inning, ASU senior outfielder Tanya Windle sparked the decisive rally with a triple into the right-center gap.

Memphis junior Marley Maness broke the shutout in the 7th with a solo home run, but it was too little, too late. "Silva was just painting the corners today," Maness admitted postgame.

Preview: The Return of Chance Stephens

UPCOMING: 2:00 PM ET • XFINITY CENTER

COLLEGE PARK, MD — The narrative writes itself. Today, Minnesota guard Chance Stephens returns to the XFINITY Center, not as a Terp, but as a Golden Gopher.

Stephens, now a redshirt junior, has become a perimeter assassin for Minnesota (13-9). He is joined by senior transfer Cade Tyson and sophomore floor general Isaac Asuma. "It's just another game," Stephens said yesterday, though few believe him.

Maryland (14-8) looks different too. The Terps are now led by Kansas transfer Rakease Passmore, whose athleticism has electrified the fanbase. In the paint, Northeastern transfer Collin Metcalf faces a tough test boxing out Minnesota's physical front line. Expect a hostile crowd welcoming their former guard home.

Super Bowl LX Preview
Seahawks
VS
Patriots

LEVI'S STADIUM, CA — The stage is set for a clash of generations and narratives. The Seattle Seahawks, led by the career renaissance of veteran quarterback Sam Darnold, face the New England Patriots and their sophomore sensation, Drake Maye.

The Air War: Darnold has thrown for over 4,000 yards this season, finally fulfilling his draft promise. His primary weapons, DK Metcalf and Jaxon Smith-Njigba, present a vertical threat that few teams can match. They will test Patriots cornerback Christian Gonzalez, who has played at an All-Pro level all year.

The Ground Game: New England’s strategy relies on keeping Darnold off the field. Running back Rhamondre Stevenson must establish the run early against a Seattle front seven coached by defensive mastermind Mike Macdonald. On the other side, Seattle’s Kenneth Walker III remains a home-run threat on every carry.

The Verdict: It comes down to mistakes. Maye has been brilliant, but this is his first Super Bowl. Darnold has the experience and the weapons. If Seattle's defense can confuse the young Patriots QB, the Lombardi Trophy heads to the Pacific Northwest.

PREDICTION: SEAHAWKS 27, PATRIOTS 24
